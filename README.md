# Advisor-AI

## Project Overview and Use Cases

AdvisorAI is meant to help students in the Five College Consortium (UMass Amherst, Amherst College, Hampshire College, Mount Holyoke College, and Smith College) better understand their degree progress and help them plan a path toward graduation. The main idea is that students can interact with the system to analyze their academic requirements and receive guidance about courses and planning decisions.

The main user of the system is a **student** who wants help understanding what they still need to graduate.

Some of the use cases for the system include:

- **Student login**  
  Students log into AdvisorAI so they can access advising tools and their academic planning dashboard.

- **Selecting institution and major**  
  Students choose their college within the Five College Consortium and select their major so the system can load the correct degree requirements.

- **Uploading Academic Requirement Report (ARR)**  
  Students upload their ARR document and provide basic information about how many semesters they have completed and how many they expect to take before graduating.

- **Generating a graduation pathway**  
  AdvisorAI analyzes the ARR and remaining degree requirements and generates a semester-by-semester plan showing how a student could complete their major.

- **Advising session summary**  
  After an advising interaction, the system generates a short summary document that students can save and review later.

- **Clarifying prerequisites and requirements**  
  Students can ask questions about course prerequisites, major requirements, or university policies.

- **Exploring accelerated graduation options**  
  Students can ask whether it is possible to graduate earlier by taking additional credits, summer classes, or adjusting their schedule.

- **Updating plans after academic changes**  
  Students can update their graduation plan if their timeline changes (for example a gap semester, co-op, study away program, or failing/withdrawing from a course).
